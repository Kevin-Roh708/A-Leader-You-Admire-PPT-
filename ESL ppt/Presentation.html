The one of the greats

Bill Gates

He is the co-founder of Microsoft and a pioneer in the software industry. He is a technology entrepreneur, investor, and philanthropist. He helped expand computer use around the world and made a big impact on global public health and education.

Yeonwoo Roh

Education or Training

He entered Harvard University, but he left school because he had a strong passion for personal computer software. He then started Microsoft with Paul Allen. From the beginning, real-world experience and project-based learning were very important to his success.

Life Accomplishments

  • Founded Microsoft in 1975 — played a key role in making personal computing popular
  • Set the standard for the software industry with the commercial success of Windows
  • Contributed to global health, education, and poverty reduction through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation
  • Continued to invest in technology and support solutions for humanity's biggest challenges

Qualities I Admire

  • Curiosity and problem-solving — always seeking innovation through technical curiosity
  • Pragmatism and execution — ability to turn ideas into real products
  • Long-term vision — continuous contribution and investment to solve social problems
  • Data-driven approach — prefers evidence-based decision making

Quote

"It's fine to celebrate success but it is more important to heed the lessons of failure."

This quote reminds us that learning from failure is more important for long-term success than just celebrating achievements.

Conclusion — Why He Inspires Me

Bill Gates combines technological innovation, practical execution, and social responsibility. His career shows the importance of curiosity, continuous learning, learning from failure, and long-term investment to solve global problems.

Lesson: Get hands-on experience in your field of interest and keep improving through results and feedback.
